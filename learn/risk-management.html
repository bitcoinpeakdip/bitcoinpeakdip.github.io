The Golden Rules of Risk Management

Rule #1: Never risk more than 2% per trade

Your total risk per trade should never exceed 2% of your trading capital.

Example: $10,000 account → Max risk = $200 per trade

Rule #2: Always use stop-loss

Every trade must have a predetermined stop-loss level.

Rule #3: Risk-Reward ratio minimum 1:2

For every $1 you risk, aim to make at least $2.

Stop-Loss Placement Strategies

1. Technical Stop-Loss

Place stop-loss just above key resistance (for shorts) or below key support (for longs).

2. ATR-Based Stop-Loss

Use Average True Range to set dynamic stops:

  • Conservative: 3x ATR
  • Moderate: 2x ATR
  • Aggressive: 1.5x ATR

3. Volatility-Adjusted Stop

Adjust stop width based on market volatility:

  • Low volatility: Tighter stops (1.5x ATR)
  • High volatility: Wider stops (3x ATR)

Take-Profit Strategy

Multi-Target Take Profit:

  • TP1 (40% position): 1.5x risk → Secure profits
  • TP2 (30% position): 2x risk → Let winners run
  • TP3 (30% position): 3x risk → Maximum potential

Maximum Exposure Limits

  • Per trade: Max 5% of capital
  • Total open positions: Max 20% of capital
  • Daily loss limit: Stop trading after 3 consecutive losses or 6% drawdown
  • Weekly loss limit: Stop trading after 10% drawdown

Remember: The goal is not to make money fast, but to preserve capital and let compounding work its magic.
